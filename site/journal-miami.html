Journal — Miami

The City
That Hums

golden hour, salt, and the beginning
The Collection · Miami, Florida

Some places keep secrets. Miami tells you hers slowly. Only if you're patient. Only if you know how to look.

I found her behind glass, in a garden that felt like it had been dreaming for a hundred years. She watched me the way I watch everything. Like she already knew I'd come back.

I've always felt a place before I understood it. The light here hums. It knows things I don't, yet.

By evening the whole sky catches fire, and no one seems surprised but me.

Golden hour is a ritual. I go down to the water when the city softens, when everything turns to glass and gold, and for one breath the whole coast holds still with me.

This is where you'll find me. Between the light and the leaving.

I won't tell you everything. Some of it stays mine. The warmer hours, the closer ones, they live behind a door I only open for a few.

But this part, the light, the salt, the beginning, this part I'll share.

Come look. And when you want to see the rest of what I keep, you already know where to find me. x
