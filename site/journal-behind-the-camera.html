The Girl Behind the Camera
(but also in front)

Words & light
Read

I'll start with a confession. You've seen me somewhere before. You just can't place it.

I was sixteen when I asked my mother to enroll me in modeling school. Neither of us had the faintest idea what I was really asking for.

I only knew I wanted to be someone. The girl on the shirt. The face on the poster. The one whose name never reaches you, but whose face you'd swear you'd met somewhere before.

A moment that lingers.

Now for the part I don't hand to just anyone.

I had cancer. Young. Too young to have the words for it.

It left something in me. A permanent tilt to the way I see. Because when you brush up against the end that early, you cannot un-know it. How much beauty is everywhere, all the time. How carelessly the rest of the world lets it slip past.

Do not mistake me for someone who always knew. It took years to learn how to look, and I got it wrong plenty. I lost the thread, wandered off, forgot the whole lesson and had to learn it twice. The seeing was never a gift. It was a practice. And I am still, happily, imperfect at it.

They spend their lives looking the other way. Chasing the shiny, forgettable things. Numbing the parts that ache instead of feeling them for what they're worth.

But some things you don't get to skip. Some suffering can't be outrun, only walked through. And the gift buried inside it, the one nobody warns you about, is this. Once you stop running, you finally start to see. The light. The body. The ordinary, aching beauty of being alive at all.

So that is why I go. Why I keep leaving.

I go looking for the parts of the world I haven't met yet. I want to stand somewhere I've never been, in a language I don't speak, and feel how small it makes me. How awake. I don't travel to collect places. I let them get into me. Let them rearrange everything I thought I knew about how a life is meant to look, and what actually makes a person happy.

The world runs on two speeds. Routine, which it calls living. Complacency, which it calls comfort. Both keep you from feeling much of anything at all.

They keep selling us a life free of pain, like that's the whole point. But what does that actually look like? Nothing that aches. Nothing to walk through. Nothing to feel at all. A flat line, and they told you to call it calm.

I'm after the third thing. Simpler. Slower. A single ripe fig. Sun scattered across the water on a warm afternoon. A stranger who waves you into their doorway like they've known you for years.

The meaning of it, this whole strange gift of being here, hides in the small quiet places. Never the loud ones.

Still Looking

And beneath all of it, I am searching.

For the place that feels familiar the moment I arrive. The one I've somehow lived a thousand times before. The one my body knows before my mind does, and calls home.

I haven't found it yet.

I think I'll know it by the way it makes my heart go quiet and glad. By the way I finally stop looking.

Until then, I travel.

So. Here's my invitation.

Come with me. Behind the photographs, past the pretty light, into the rooms I don't open for everyone. Who I really am. Unfiltered. Unbothered. Unapologetic.

I know exactly what I am. Confident. Bold. Too much for some, and perfectly at peace with that. I walk my own path, and I keep the ones who care to walk it beside me.

And here is what drives me, the whole truth of it. Somewhere along the way, we forgot that a story is a kind of spell. So I'm gathering mine back up. The places, the moments, the beautiful strange magic of it all, and giving them somewhere to live. A whole little world, told one picture at a time.

Why don't you tag along?

If you'd rather not, that's quite alright. No one forced you through the door.

But if you've stayed with me this long, I suspect we're the same kind of trouble.

So come find me. I'll be here, chasing the light.

You know you want to.

xo, Eleanora
