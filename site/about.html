The alter ego

Eleanora is the most perfect version of me. Confident, bold, unapologetic. She isn't a mask I put on. She's me, fully expressed.

Modeling was never about pretending to be someone else. It was the one place I could be more myself, on my own terms. This is where I finally collect it all — the travels, the escapades, the beautiful and the unapologetic.

on falling for the ritual
i.

The dream came before the skill

I first got into modeling at sixteen. I asked my mom if I could sign up for Barbizon, because I always really wanted to be a model. She said yes. I didn't learn much there — but I started meeting people, and by around twenty I was doing real shoots.

The dream was there long before I knew what I was doing.

So I let the learning come from the doing. From the light. From showing up.

ii.

The camera never exposed me

As a teenager I felt awkward and weird, and I hated having my picture taken. But in front of the lens I could take my time — express my strange, particular self in a way that was curated. A way that made me feel beautiful on the inside, too.

It didn't expose me. It gave me a place to finally feel beautiful.

iii.

Why this is art, not content

I've worked with everyone from the most amateur to the ones who make real art, and the difference is everything. A great one sees the light and the shadows and helps you fine-tune the perfect frame.

There's a cadence to it. Every few seconds you hit the pose with the full emotion in the eyes — and it's hard to hold. The best ones feel that moment coming, and they're ready to catch it.

That waiting for the honest second — that's the whole art.

The mission
The woman's body is beautiful, and I hate how much we shame it. this is my way of sticking it to the man.

This is life, and I think it's beautiful. If you don't want to look — close your eyes, turn around, and walk the other way.
