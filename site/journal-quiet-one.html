The Way I See It · a manifesto

I've always been the quiet one. But I was just busy building my road map.

stay close
Words & light · piece by piece
Read

I was handed the map early. School, then college, then the job, the ring, the house, the kids, the desk you sit at until they finally let you leave. Everyone around me walked it like it was the only road.

Then an opportunity showed up. The kind you have to be a little reckless to take. I took the risk.

Halfway through my second year of college, I dropped out. Walked out of the plan to go find my own.

But that wasn't the first time I had questioned the M.O. It happened in the pews too. I was raised in the Christian Church, and I sat there wondering what the fuck was happening.

It felt like a room built to make me feel guilty, and then passed a basket shaming families into donating to them.

None of it made sense. As soon as my mother told me I had a choice whether or not I went, I was gone like a fish in the sea.

My defiance of authority, of what everyone calls the right way, it started young.

I've spent the better part of twenty years asking the questions no one around me was asking.

Where do I belong?
Who are my people?
Why did I get to survive?
What is this all for?

I didn't always know I was searching. But the feeling never stopped.

And slowly, the pieces started falling into place. I saw myself as someone who traveled. Then I actually started traveling. I saw myself as more than a minimum wage employee. Then I taught myself to become a manager.

None of it was random. It was day by day. Step by step. Rooted in logic, not magic.

There's a protocol. Follow it, and you can have anything you want. It's that simple. And it's the hardest thing you'll ever do.

Most people strive to be in the 1%. Not me. Find me among the happiest.

The ones who woke up, chose their own game, and finally walked through the door. I feel closer to the answer than I've ever been. Closer every day.

That reckless risk I took all those years ago? I couldn't be happier that I did.

I built the map out of my own life. And now I'm going to hand it to you, piece by piece.

The door is opening. Stay close.

The magic's already begun. You just can't see it yet.

xo, Eleanora
