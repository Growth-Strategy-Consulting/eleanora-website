The rooms share the same bones (Ink, Cream, Sand, Terracotta). Color reads in relationship, not isolation — palettes are judged on the rendered page, never as loose swatches (Albers). Cream on Forest/Pine passes WCAG AA; gold + blush are for large display or fills, never small body text.

4

Art Direction

Golden-hour, film-grade. Global wash saturate(1.04) contrast(1.02) brightness(1.02) sepia(.05). Subjects: the female form as art (tasteful, never apology) + travel, water, greenhouse. Give heroes calm negative space so type stays legible. One focal element, generous space, the brand's own furniture only — no stock, no clip-art (Rams).

5

Voice

"I think life is so beautiful it feels wrong to keep it to myself. So I don't. Come in, stay a while, take your time. There's no hurry here."

Mystical · earthy · whimsical · unbothered  ·  short lines, concrete nouns, no em dashes, elusive, never salesy. Sign-off: xo, Eleanora
6

Do & Don't

Do

  • Lead with calligraphy for the wordmark and emotional lines.
  • Keep one accent per room; let the palette shift like walking rooms.
  • Give every hero calm negative space for the text.
  • Use the sprig + seal as the only ornament (it's the house furniture).
  • Check cream/ink on any tint against WCAG 4.5:1.

Don't

  • Sexy stays subtle & self-possessed; never a thirst trap. Archive, not content.
  • Don't set body or long text in calligraphy (illegible).
  • Don't cramp the seal's arc lettering — space it evenly.
  • Don't add a stray color (no gold outside its role, no new hues).
  • Don't place light type over busy photos.
