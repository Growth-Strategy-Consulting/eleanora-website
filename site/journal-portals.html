I Think I've Been Collecting Portals

where do you go?
A time capsule · left open on purpose
Read

Small confession. I have a thing for doors.

Not new doors. Old ones. Ancient ones. The heavy wooden kind with the paint peeling off, the ones that have been standing in some little street longer than my whole country has been a country. Wherever I go, I find them. And I stop, and I take the picture. Every single time. I have hundreds of them by now. A whole camera roll of doors that open into houses I will never step inside.

I never really thought about why. It was just a thing I did. The girl who stops for every old door.

But I'm sitting here tonight going back through all of them, and something funny hit me. What if I was never photographing doors at all. What if some quiet part of me has always seen them as little portals. Doorways into other dimensions. Other versions of the story. Other lives. Maybe I've been standing in front of all these entrances my whole life with my camera up, quietly asking each one the same thing. Where do you go?

Humor me for a second, because it gets better.

Where do you go?

My whole life is turning into a doorway now. This thing I'm building, this year I'm giving myself, it's just another door I'm walking through with no idea what's on the other side. Same as always. Camera up. Where do you go.

So maybe it was never a hobby. Maybe it was a hint. A little sign I kept leaving for myself, over and over, in a hundred different cities, long before I was ready to read it.

xo, Eleanora
