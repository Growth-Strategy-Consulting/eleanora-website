01 · Brand Essence

the premise

Beauty, the way
she sees it.

Eleanora is a private-feeling archive made public: photography, travel, womanhood, places with secrets, and the honest moments found between the beautiful ones.

The identity should feel discovered rather than advertised. Feminine without fragility. Mystical without costume. Luxurious because it is considered, not because it is polished flat.

03 · Distinctive Brand Assets

the recognizable world

Assets that make Eleanora recognizable without the logo.

Distinctive Brand Assets are memory cues. Repeat this small set consistently until any one of them can make someone think Eleanora before the name appears.

01Primary

The Garden Arch

The doorway is the brand's most ownable shape: an invitation into a private archive, a garden, or a story not yet told.

RULE  Use as a frame, portal, crop, or reveal, not as generic ornament.

02Primary

The Gilded Spark

A tiny four-point star behaves like a glint caught in late light. It signals discovery, wonder, and something worth noticing.

RULE  One to three sparks per composition. Never turn it into glitter wallpaper.

03Primary

The Pressed Sprig

Fine botanical linework ties the archive to real plants, collected places, and the wild without becoming bohemian.

RULE  Favor delicate specimens, asymmetry, and negative space.

04Narrative

The Open Door

Language about doors, rooms, thresholds, keys, and invitations creates a repeatable world across launches and captions.

RULE  Make the invitation intimate, never exclusionary or theatrical.

05Graphic

The Golden Trail

A thin rule, reflection, or line of gold guides the eye through layouts the way sunlight travels across water.

RULE  Keep it fine and directional; gold is a verb here, not a fill color.

06Editorial

The Field Note

Small uppercase labels, place names, dates, and chapter markers make every piece feel catalogued inside a living archive.

RULE  Use wide tracking, concise language, and documentary specificity.

07Motion

The Veil

Slow dissolves, soft reveals, mist, grain, and shadow make browsing feel like entering a memory instead of loading a page.

RULE  Movement should be almost imperceptible and always respect reduced motion.

08Photography

The Honest Frame

Real plants, real light, skin, water, architecture, and the slightly imperfect moment when the image begins to feel true.

RULE  Cinematic and tactile; never glossy stock, sterile luxury, or heavy fantasy effects.

06 · Image World

real plants · real light

Photograph the moment the image goes honest.

Let shadows stay. Keep air around the subject. Choose weather, texture, movement, age, and natural imperfection over anything overly staged.
